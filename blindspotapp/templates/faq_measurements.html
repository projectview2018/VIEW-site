The visibility scores are the percent of the volume of interest that is visible to the driver. Below is an image of a slice of volume of interest in the front.

The volume of interest is the volume around the vehicle extending fifteen feet forward from the end of the hood, fifteen feet from the side from the passenger side of the vehicle, and 74 inches high. The volume extending from the driver side is not measured. Crash data has shown that crash risks from driver side blindzones are minimal compared to the other sides.

The app's blind area projection (nearest visible points on the ground) has been validated on a test vehicle to be within X percent of real-world physical measurements. Further validation is planned, and we welcome anyone interested in partnering with us to do so with additional vehicles.

The front and passenger side blindzones have different crash risk percents. In order to account for this, the front visibility and side visibility scores have been added to the overall visibility score.

A vulnerable road user is considered invisible if a driver cannot see their head. The shoulder heights of 5th percentile United States female vulnerable road users are used in this calculation.
